Research

Current topics in optical fiber interferometers, hollow-core fibers, and magnetic-fluid-based sensing.

Magnetic-Fluid-Based Fiber Magnetic Field Sensors

My recent work focuses on fiber Fabry–Pérot and anti-resonant structures filled with magnetic fluid for magnetic field sensing. By exploiting the tunable refractive index and anisotropy of magnetic fluids under parallel and perpendicular magnetic fields, I design compact fiber structures with high sensitivity and direction-dependent spectral responses.

Hollow-Core Fiber Mach–Zehnder Interferometers

I am also engaged in the design and analysis of fiber Mach–Zehnder interferometers (FMZIs) based on hollow-core fibers with different core diameters. By connecting discrepant hollow-core sections, it is possible to achieve ultrahigh extinction ratios, ultrabroadband single-dip spectra, and improved sensing performance for temperature and refractive index measurements.

Optical Measurement & Data Analysis

In addition to device fabrication, I work on optical spectrum acquisition, curve fitting, and signal processing using tools such as MATLAB. These methods are used to evaluate sensitivity, extinction ratio, and stability of the proposed fiber sensors, as well as to model the magneto-optical behavior of magnetic fluids.
