Short Biography

I am currently an undergraduate student majoring in electro-optical engineering. My research focuses on optical fiber interferometers, anti-resonant guiding mechanisms, and the tunable refractive index of magnetic fluids for high-sensitivity magnetic field sensing.

I have hands-on experience in synthesizing magnetic fluids, building Helmholtz-coil magnetic field systems, and designing fiber Fabry–Pérot and Mach–Zehnder interferometers. I actively participate in academic conferences and student paper competitions to present my work and improve my scientific communication skills.

Research Interests

  • Optical fiber interferometers (Mach–Zehnder, Fabry–Pérot, FMZI)
  • Hollow-core fibers and anti-resonant reflecting optical waveguides (ARROW)
  • Magnetic-fluid refractive index tuning and anisotropy under external magnetic fields
  • Fiber-based magnetic field sensors with tunable sensitivity and vector response
  • Optical spectrum analysis, curve fitting, and sensing performance evaluation
