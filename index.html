Short Biography

I am currently an undergraduate student in electro-optical engineering, with research interests in optical fiber interferometers, anti-resonant guiding mechanisms, and the tunable refractive index of magnetic fluids for high-sensitivity magnetic field sensing. I have experience in synthesizing magnetic fluids, building Helmholtz-coil magnetic field systems, and designing fiber Fabry–Pérot and Mach–Zehnder interferometers.

I actively participate in academic conferences and competitions, presenting my work on fiber magnetic field sensors and hollow-core fiber interferometers, and I am continuously improving my skills in optical measurements, data analysis, and scientific communication.

Research Interests

  • Optical fiber interferometers (Mach–Zehnder, Fabry–Pérot, FMZI)
  • Hollow-core fibers and anti-resonant reflecting optical waveguides (ARROW)
  • Magnetic-fluid refractive index tuning and anisotropy under external magnetic fields
  • Fiber-based magnetic field sensors with tunable sensitivity and vector response
  • Optical spectrum analysis and data fitting for sensing applications
